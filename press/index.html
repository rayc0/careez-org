About CareEZ

Company overview

CareEZ is a Hong Kong clinical AI platform purpose-built for dysphagia management and care-food (照護食) standard adoption in residential and community eldercare settings. It is operated by Our parent social enterprise, a HKCSS-registered social enterprise (SE Tree Mark #83) founded to address one of Hong Kong's most urgent and under-resourced preventable health crises: aspiration pneumonia arising from undetected swallowing impairment in frail elderly residents.

Dysphagia — impaired swallowing — affects an estimated 28,000 to 42,000 residents of Hong Kong's 700+ residential care homes for the elderly (RCHEs) at any given moment, and a further 150,000 elderly individuals in the community. Every meal-safety decision at Hong Kong's care facilities is made not by a speech therapist or doctor, but by a frontline carer — one of approximately 640,000 people performing that role daily, the majority of whom have no structured dysphagia training and no clinical tool in their language.

CareEZ closes that gap. The platform integrates text, image, and voice modalities to deliver real-time dysphagia risk screening and 照護食 texture classification, fully aligned with the HKCSS 護食標準 (Hong Kong's nationally recognised care-food standard) and the international IDDSI framework (International Dysphagia Diet Standardisation Initiative). Clinical outputs are governed by a safety protocol anchored at the HKU Swallowing Research Laboratory and independently stewarded through Full Linkage Community Care Association, a Hong Kong Section 88 registered charity that holds the public clinical dataset outside any commercial balance sheet.

The parent social enterprise's product reach is established, not aspirational. The SeniorDeli care-food line — 12 SKUs in active distribution through Getz Healthcare — is already present across 200+ partner care facilities in Hong Kong. The founder holds the HKSAR TechUp Y06 grant (Innovation and Technology Commission) and is in residence at the HKU TEC iDendron Seed Programme (cohort HKU25-05) through the sister project PQSafe. Our parent social enterprise is a co-drafting institution of T/SATA 094-2025, the Greater Bay Area group standard on General Requirements for Dysphagia Foods for Elderly Care, and is named in companion standards T/SATA 084-2025 and T/SATA 085-2025.

CareEZ operates in Cantonese, English, Tagalog, and Bahasa Indonesia — the four languages of Hong Kong's frontline care workforce — and is deployable at the point of care via mobile browser, with no installation required. A live API is publicly accessible at demo.careez.org and api.seniordeli.com. The 照護食 Adoption Index — an independent, methodology-transparent benchmarking instrument for measuring care-food standard adoption across operators — is published at careez.org/adoption-index/.

CareEZ is seeking HKEX Impact and Sustainability Fund (IFS) 2026 support to fund a 30-site RCHE pilot and the deployment of multilingual Adoption Champions across Hong Kong's care facility network, targeting measurable reduction in aspiration pneumonia incidence and full IDDSI compliance across participating operators within 24 months of grant award.

About our parent social enterprise

Our parent social enterprise — Founder & brand assets

Social Enterprise — SE Tree Mark #83

Our Parent Social Enterprise

Our parent social enterprise is the Hong Kong company behind CareEZ, incorporated as a social enterprise and registered on the HKCSS Social Enterprise Directory (SE Tree Mark #83). Its mission is to make evidence-based dysphagia management tools available to every frontline carer in Hong Kong, regardless of language or institutional setting.

Our parent social enterprise operates the SeniorDeli care-food product line — 12 IDDSI-certified SKUs distributed through Getz Healthcare across 200+ partner care facilities — and the CareEZ clinical AI platform, which extends the same clinical evidence base into a real-time digital screening and classification tool. The company is a co-drafting institution of the GBA dysphagia food group standard T/SATA 094-2025 and maintains an active grant relationship with the Hong Kong Innovation and Technology Commission (TechUp Y06).

FL
Full Linkage Community Care Association
Independent Data Steward
Hong Kong Section 88 registered charity

Full Linkage Community Care Association is a registered Hong Kong s.88 charity and the independent co-steward of the public clinical resource created by the CareEZ programme. It is a separate legal entity with its own board, governance, and audited accounts, partnered with the HKU Swallowing Research Laboratory.

The co-steward structure answers the governance question every institutional reviewer asks: who owns the clinical dataset, and what prevents its privatisation? Full Linkage's s.88 status anchors the public-interest disposition of all data. Our parent social enterprise operates as the delivery vehicle; the clinical resource sits outside its balance sheet.
